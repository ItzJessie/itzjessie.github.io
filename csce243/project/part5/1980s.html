Japanese Animation History Archive

1980s

Key Developments

  • Mecha, cyberpunk, and shounen titles like Gundam and Dragon Ball rise in popularity
  • OVA (Original Video Animation) launches, allowing for more experimental and mature content
  • High-budget, artistically ambitious films like Akira push the boundaries of animation
  • Anime gains widespread, international audiences as it moves beyond Japan

Influential Series

1982 - Super Dimension Fortress Macross

A spacefaring mecha series that pioneered variable fighters and romantic drama.

1984 - Dragon Ball

The iconic start of an anime mega-franchise focused on martial arts and adventure.

1984 - Zeta Gundam

A darker, more complex sequel that deepened the Gundam universe's lore.
