Japanese Animation History Archive

1980s

Key Developments

  • Mecha, cyberpunk, and shounen titles like Gundam and Dragon Ball rise in popularity
  • OVA (Original Video Animation) launches, allowing for more experimental and mature content
  • High-budget, artistically ambitious films like Akira push the boundaries of animation
  • Anime gains widespread, international audiences as it moves beyond Japan

Influential Series

1982 - Super Dimension Fortress Macross

A spacefaring mecha series that pioneered variable fighters and romantic drama.

1984 - Dragon Ball

The iconic start of an anime mega-franchise focused on martial arts and adventure.

1984 - Zeta Gundam

A darker, more complex sequel that deepened the Gundam universe's lore.

1990s

Key Developments

  • The TV anime boom expands genres, from psychological drama to stylish space westerns
  • Studio-driven hits like Evangelion reshape storytelling and character archetypes
  • High-profile films like Princess Mononoke prove anime can dominate the box office
  • Global fandom grows through cable TV, VHS, and the early internet

Influential Series

1995 - Neon Genesis Evangelion

A landmark series that blended mecha action with psychological character study.

1997 - Princess Mononoke

A sweeping film epic that pushed anime into mainstream global conversation.

1998 - Cowboy Bebop

A genre-blending space western known for cinematic direction and music.

Japanese Animation History Archive

2000s

Key Developments

  • Digital production pipelines become the standard, speeding up TV schedules and expanding visual styles
  • Long-running shounen hits like Naruto, One Piece, and Bleach dominate global fandoms
  • Light novel and manga adaptations fuel a wave of genre variety, from dark thrillers to school dramas
  • International releases grow through DVDs and early streaming, widening access beyond Japan

Influential Series

2001 - Spirited Away

An Academy Award-winning film that brought global attention to anime's storytelling power.

2004 - Fullmetal Alchemist

A richly plotted fantasy series that blended action, philosophy, and sibling drama.

2006 - Death Note

A tense cat-and-mouse thriller that became a breakout hit worldwide.

Japanese Animation History Archive

2010s

Key Developments

  • Streaming platforms and simulcasts make new episodes available worldwide, nearly day-and-date with Japan
  • Digital pipelines and compositing become the default, accelerating production and enabling hybrid 2D/3D visuals
  • The isekai boom reshapes TV lineups, while seasonal cour formats dominate scheduling
  • Anime becomes a mainstream global entertainment category with large conventions and social media fandoms

Influential Series

2013 - Attack on Titan

A breakout dark fantasy series that helped define the decade's global anime conversation.

2011 - Puella Magi Madoka Magica

A genre-bending magical girl series known for its darker themes and stylish direction.

2019 - Demon Slayer

A blockbuster shounen hit praised for its cinematic action and stunning animation.

2020s

Key Developments

  • Streaming-first releases and global simulcasts make anime a weekly worldwide event
  • Visual standards climb with hybrid 2D/3D workflows and cinematic compositing
  • Major franchises expand into films, games, and theme experiences with coordinated campaigns
  • Global fandom accelerates, with social platforms shaping breakout hits

Influential Series

2020 - Jujutsu Kaisen

A modern shounen milestone known for kinetic battles and cinematic animation.

2021 - ODDTAXI

A noir mystery that spotlighted tight writing and mature, grounded storytelling.

2022 - Cyberpunk: Edgerunners

A stylish, neon-drenched series that helped drive global interest in studio collaborations.
